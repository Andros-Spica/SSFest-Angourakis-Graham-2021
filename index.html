Agent-based modelling in Archaeology



Many and very diverse applications
Agent-based modelling is a relatively extended practice in Archaeology. There has been many and very diverse applications for more than 20 years.

Big challenge

Complicated designs + lack of documentation

=

Articles are read, results interpreted...

but no one engages with the models

Still, ABM remains a niche community in archaeology, given a big fundamental challenge: | the combination of complicated designs and lack of documentation | results in agent-based models being effectively | black boxes. | Articles are read, results interpreted... | but no one engages with the models.

pseudo code, UML, ODD, DSG...
there is no lack of intent!
And there has been no lack of intent in trying to solve this problem from inside and outside archaeology.
